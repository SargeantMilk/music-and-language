Syntax

Languages have syntactic rules which determine how meaning elements at different levels can legally fit together, forming morphemes into words and words into sentences. These rules are different across different languages, but they are ultimately constrained by the need to produce something in which the meaning is interpretable to a fairly precise degree. Constructions that do not follow these rules can end up with a different meaning from what was intended(as in example 2), or even be entirely uninterpretable (example 3).

Likewise, music has rules that determine the legal construction of individual notes into phrases, and phrases into longer sequences (Patel, 2008). Despite not having the same pressure for communication, deviation from these rules can result in phrases that leave a different impression, feel unfinished or unpleasant to listen to.
